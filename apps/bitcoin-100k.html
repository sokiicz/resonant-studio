What is this?

In November 2024, Bitcoin crossed $100,000 for the first time. For many people who had followed, believed in, or just been curious about Bitcoin for years, it was a surreal moment. This page is a snapshot of that feeling.

It's not a financial tool, not an investment platform, not a tracker. It's a commemorative page — built quickly, in the heat of the moment, as a celebration. Simple, direct, fun.

The title

"Jeuz Bitcoin za 100K" is a casual, excited exclamation — roughly translating to "Jesus, Bitcoin at 100K!" It captures the energy of that moment perfectly: disbelief mixed with excitement.

Why include it here?

Because not every project needs to be a serious productivity tool. Some apps are just about capturing a moment and sharing a feeling. This is one of those. It shows that Claude Code can build anything — including a piece of digital folk art.

The idea

Bitcoin hit $100K. I wanted to make something to mark the occasion. Not a dashboard, not data — just a page that said "this happened, and it felt like this." A digital postcard from a historic moment.

Building it

I described what I wanted to Claude Code: a celebratory web page marking Bitcoin crossing $100K, with a punchy title, some visual flair, and a sense of occasion. It took maybe 20 minutes from idea to a deployed page.

That speed is the point. This kind of thing — capturing a fleeting cultural moment — only works if you can build it fast. Claude Code makes that possible for anyone with an idea.
