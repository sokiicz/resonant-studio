The story behind Resonant Labs

From frustrated experimenter
to shipping apps daily

I've been fascinated by AI since the early days — and the moment coding capabilities arrived, I was in. Screenshot a bug, paste it to the AI, wait, try again. Lose the memory window, watch the AI break working things while fixing new ones. For someone with no coding background, it was one step forward, two steps back.

Then came Perplexity, then Google AI Studio — closer each time, but still fragile. Add something new, break something old. Then Claude Code arrived and everything changed. It holds context, understands the whole project, iterates without unravelling what already works. I'm now finishing real, working apps at a pace that still surprises me.

To be clear: it's not self-working. I still think through every concept, guide each step, and shape the vision start to finish. But instead of writing code, I do it through conversation — my thinking, my instincts, my ideas. The AI handles the implementation. That separation is everything.

Since the AI wrote the code, charging for it felt dishonest. So everything here is free and open source, MIT licensed — for anyone to use, fork, or build on.
